Holistic Healing

By: Akshata Tar

Often more than anytime you feel the need to pause and answer the question rising from deep within, Why? before it is dismissed as an errant and you continue...


on your race, against your self. The need to figure out the What, Which, How and Why of your self is ridiculed, is marked as unnecessary, and those who do want to pursue the need are deemed as weak or as “mentals” by their surrounding peers.

Health has never had an only physical attribute, it does have our mental state associated with it too. Without balance between our body, our mind and our soul, we are never healthy, regardless of whether we accept it or not. And it is time, that we do fully accept it, for times ever keeps changing and keep being difficult, and we need to fully incorporate within ourselves the true meaning of health, in order to be happy and peaceful in true sense.

We, as individuals, are different and unique, in every aspect, from the very genes constituting us to our personalities. And still, we are all connected and are One! Though we are a part of something bigger, each of us exists to play a certain role and we need to interconnect harmoniously, externally and internally to survive as, well, us! As your true selves!

Why do we need holistic healing?
Because our physical ailments or sufferings aren’t just physical anymore! There’s much more to it, much deeper level affections of which we are ignorant or tend to dismiss them, again. We, as a whole, have our body, our mind and our spirit to be taken care of, failing to tend to even one, disrupts the balance and brings up diseases.

“A healthy outside starts from the inside”
- Robert Urich



Our bodies have natural healing capabilities and when hurt, it shows us, through physical manifestations. All we need is to pay attention, listen and understand why.

In order to live a healthy life, we need to have balance within ourselves first. With our selves. With self. And the journey towards accepting our own self and loving it goes far more than just knowing our talents and strengths. The journey takes a sharp derail when we begin to falter around our fears, our faults, our desires because it is at this point, that your mind starts to protect yourself from facing the shadow, a step you must though take, in order to understand your self truly. Without knowing the negatives, you don’t know the positives, and without knowing your fears and faults, you will not truly marvel in your strengths and your individuality. A person who does not shed a single tear is not strong, the one who allows himself to grieve is. Understanding your imperfections, reflecting truly on your emotions, and accepting them as a part of yourself is self-awareness. And when you begin to understand yourself, at a much deeper level, you become self-aware, and then you truly begin to heal yourself.

Healing isn’t easy, it’s hard. It isn’t beautiful, it’s rough. It doesn’t happen overnight, it takes time.
But caterpillar who thought she’ll die in the cocoon, emerged as a beautiful butterfly, ready to soar the world! Health has always been important but today, tending to your inner self is the first step towards a healthy lifestyle. When your mind and spirit is at calm, it will reflect on body physically. Your health will be seen and felt by others only then.

Holistic healing is an approach to life, to put yourself first, in order to spread the love that overflows from you to others. It is a choice, to free yourself from the weights that pull you down, to fly free in any direction you want to. It is a teacher, from which wise, uncountable lessons are need to learnt, the primary being, accept and love yourself first! It is path of life, which will only lead you to true happiness and peace, starting with yourself first.

“Wounds don’t heal the way you want them to, they heal the way they need to. It takes time for the wounds to heal into scars. It takes time for the process of healing to take place. Give yourself that time. Give yourself that grace. Be gentle with your wounds. Be gentle with your heart.

You deserve to heal.”

--Akshata Tar
